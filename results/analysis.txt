MLE Estimation Results

Analytical estimate:
λ = 12.14 cars per minute

Numerical estimate using optimization:
λ = 12.14 cars per minute

Both analytical and numerical approaches produce the same result,
confirming that the Maximum Likelihood Estimator for a Poisson
distribution equals the sample mean.

Visualization Interpretation

The histogram of the observed traffic data was compared with the
Poisson probability mass function.

The shape of the Poisson distribution closely follows the pattern
of the observed data, suggesting that the Poisson model provides a
reasonable approximation for traffic flow.

Outlier Analysis

If an incorrect observation such as 200 vehicles is added to the
dataset, the mean changes drastically:

Original mean ≈ 12.14  
New mean ≈ 24.67

This demonstrates that Maximum Likelihood Estimation is sensitive
to outliers because it depends directly on the arithmetic mean.

In real-world city planning, such an error could cause incorrect
traffic predictions and lead to unnecessary infrastructure
decisions such as widening roads.
